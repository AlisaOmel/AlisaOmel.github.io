About me

I'm a fourth-year PhD candidate in the CMU-Pitt Joint Computational Biology program, advised by Dr. Jishnu Das. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and biology applying interaction-based language models, multi-omic network analysis, and protein modeling, in immunology and disease.

Before my PhD, I worked in drug discovery at a biotech startup and then as a lab manager in an academic neuroscience lab. It was in that academic setting that I recognized how computational tools and workflows can break down when they lose touch with the biology and the scientific audience they're meant to serve. That's what drove me to pursue a PhD. I wanted to build methods that are computationally rigorous and biologically relevant. Every benchmark, model, or pipeline I build gets tested against that standard.

Outside research, I mentor future scientists, advocate for science and education policy, and communicate scientific topics to the public.

What I'm focused on right now

  • Designing benchmarks and evaluation criteria that measure biological relevance
  • Building reproducible tools and pipelines that other researchers can use
  • Interaction language models (iLMs) for peptide, protein, and small-molecule interactions
  • Applying network and systems approaches to integrate biological information and fuel translational discovery in an unbiased manner
  • Patient-specific neo-epitope prediction and prioritization in pediatric cancer
